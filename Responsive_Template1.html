UNDERSTAND THE NEWS A landmark nuclear deal was signed between Iran and the US in the year 2015. This deal was termed as The Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (JCPOA). This deal was among the P5+1(the permanent members of the United Nations Security Council the United States, the United Kingdom, Russia, France, and China plus Germany), the European Union and the Islamic Republic of Iran. According to this plan, Iran agreed to eliminate its stockpile of medium-enriched uranium. It also was to cut its stockpile of low-enriched uranium by 98%. Lastly, it was to reduce its gas centrifuges by 98% for 13 years.Trump ended the US commitment to the Iran nuclear deal exactly one year ago. On Wednesday, Iranian President Hassan Rouhani announced two steps partly to end his country's commitment to the accord. First, Iran will once again start stockpiling extra low-enriched uranium and heavy water. Secondly, Rouhani has given the signatories of eth deal a 60-days duration to end US-led financial pressure on Iran's oil and banking sectors. This clearly states that Iran wants a new deal to be made.
